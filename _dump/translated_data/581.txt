In the upcoming elections in March, candidates may be elected to the municipal council who have insufficient knowledge of spatial planning. Moreover, in a few years' time, municipalities will be responsible for water policy, but they may not be adequately prepared for it. As a result, not enough attention could be paid to the importance of drinking water and water extraction fields. This is what Jelle Hannema, director of the Gelderland water company Vitens, says, which provides drinking water to 5.6 million Dutch people.

The new Environmental Act will come into effect from 2021. This law places a much greater task and responsibility on municipalities when it comes to spatial development. Hannema: "That is a serious point for us: will drinking water interests be sufficiently taken into account in spatial decisions?" Region 12
