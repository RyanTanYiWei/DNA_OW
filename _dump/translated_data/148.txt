Elbert Joosten, prospective councilor of the CDA, is the only new council member to be sworn in today in the municipality of Horst aan de Maas. His three colleagues have already served four years.
The new municipal council of Horst aan de Maas will make do with one less councilor for the next four years. So not five aldermen, but four. Together with Mayor Ryan Palmen - who is responsible for, among other things, communication, public order and safety and integrity - they must divide the work.
The new (currently intended) councilor Elbert Joosten, who was put forward for this position by the CDA after it became known that the party does not want to continue with sitting councilor Han Geurts, will be responsible for sports, art and culture, finance and club life, among other things. in his portfolio. Veteran Thijs Kuipers (D66-GroenLinks) will be responsible for, among other things, the environmental law, enforcement and supervision and he remains the man for the energy transition and sustainability.
Roy Bouten (PvdA) will be responsible for health and care, youth and education, work and income and (integration of) labor migrants and other new residents. His colleague Eric Beurskens (Essence) will be responsible for matters such as climate adaptation, spatial planning, economics, Greenport and other industrial estates, construction and land policy over the next four years.
The new councilors will be officially nominated by their party tonight during a specially scheduled council meeting and then appointed.
